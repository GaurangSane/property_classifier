Text is lowercased and punctuation is replaced with whitespace rather than deleted; a letter-digit boundary rule additionally splits glued tokens like "Flat3B" and "A604" into separate terms so the vectorizer sees "flat" and "3" instead of one unrecoverable token. Six numeric/binary features are computed per-row from the raw address (address_length, has_pincode, math_density, is_flat_kw, is_comm_kw, is_land_kw) plus a seventh, has_structure, added to separate built-structure listings from pure land-registry entries that share the same survey-number vocabulary. All engineered features and the StandardScaler are fit on the training split only; validation and test only ever see transform().

Model is LightGBM (multiclass, 800 estimators, lr=0.04, num_leaves=63, early stopping patience=50) on the hstack of word TF-IDF (1-2 gram, 30k features), char TF-IDF (3-5 gram, 30k features), and the 7 scaled numeric/keyword features. Class imbalance is handled with inverse-frequency sample weights. Split is 70/15/15 stratified, SEED=42. Inference (app.py) applies a keyword-based override when the top prediction is "others" but a shorthand flag fired; the override runs before the displayed confidence is read, so the number shown always matches the returned label, and every override is written to override_audit.log for traceability.

Test set (n=1771, pre-has_structure baseline): flat 0.941 F1, commercial unit 0.931, houseorplot 0.873, others 0.841, landparcel 0.779; accuracy 0.888, macro F1 0.8729. landparcel/houseorplot is the primary confusion pair — both rely on survey/khasra vocabulary with no reliable structure signal to separate them, which has_structure targets directly; re-run train.py to get post-fix numbers before sign-off.
